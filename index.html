About Me

A computational biologist at the intersection of molecular science and data analytics

I recently graduated with a Master's degree in Bioinformatics and Molecular Biochemistry from George Washington University, bringing a strong foundation in biotechnology and extensive hands-on experience in RNA sequencing analysis.

My research focuses on inflammation, fibrosis, and therapeutic modulation in conditions such as Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy and COVID-19. I specialize in designing and executing comprehensive omics studies including bulk RNA-seq, single-cell RNA-seq, spatial transcriptomics, and metagenomics.

With publications in prestigious journals covering topics from Alzheimer's disease to microbial fuel cells, I bring a multidisciplinary approach to scientific inquiry. My diverse skill set combines biochemical techniques with computational pipelines, cloud computing, and advanced bioinformatics tools.

Currently positioned at the intersection of biological research and data analysis, I'm passionate about contributing to translational and precision medicine initiatives. Work authorization: F-1 OPT till July 2026, STEM OPT extension eligible.
